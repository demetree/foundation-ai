ONNX Runtime is Microsoft's cross-platform inference engine for models exported to the ONNX (Open Neural Network Exchange) interchange format. Originally focused on classical machine learning and computer vision, in recent years it has grown a generative AI pipeline called ORT GenAI that handles transformer-style autoregressive token generation, including KV-cache management, sampling strategies, and tool-calling. The runtime supports a wide range of execution providers, including CPU, CUDA, DirectML on Windows, CoreML on Apple devices, TensorRT for NVIDIA-specific optimisation, and OpenVINO for Intel hardware -- chosen at session-create time without code changes. Its popularity in the .NET ecosystem comes from first-class managed bindings that let you load and run a 4-bit-quantised Phi-3 or Phi-4 model from C# in a few dozen lines, with no Python interpreter or external service required. Models for many popular open-weight architectures are pre-converted and published on HuggingFace.

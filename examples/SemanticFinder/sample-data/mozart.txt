Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was an Austrian composer of the classical period, born in Salzburg in 1756 to a violinist and music teacher who recognised his prodigious talent before he was five. Touring the courts of Europe as a child performer with his sister Nannerl, he produced his first symphonies at age eight and his first operas in his early teens. His mature work spans every important genre of his time: more than forty symphonies including the great trio of 1788, twenty-seven piano concertos that essentially invented the form, a sequence of operas in collaboration with the librettist Lorenzo Da Ponte (The Marriage of Figaro, Don Giovanni, and Cosi fan tutte) that remain repertory cornerstones, the singspiel Die Zauberflote, and a Requiem left unfinished at his death in Vienna in 1791 at the age of thirty-five. The combination of melodic invention, formal balance, and emotional depth in his late work has been admired without interruption ever since.
